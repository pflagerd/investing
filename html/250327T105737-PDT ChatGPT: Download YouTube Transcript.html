Yes, there are a few ways to download the transcript of a YouTube video, depending on whether the video has captions (either auto-generated or manually added). Here are some simple methods:

🔹 Method 1: Use YouTube's Built-in Transcript Viewer

  1. Open the YouTube video.

  2. Click the three dots (⋮) under the video or click the "More" button (next to Save).

  3. Choose “Show transcript” (if available).

  4. A transcript panel will open with timestamps.

  5. You can copy and paste it into a text editor.

    • To remove timestamps, just copy only the text part or paste it into a text editor and use find/replace or regex to clean it up.

🔹 Method 2: Use a Website Tool

  • Try online services like:

    • https://downsub.com

    • https://transcripts.foretube.app

    • https://www.youtubetranscript.com

You just paste the YouTube URL, and it gives you a downloadable transcript (if available).

🔹 Method 3: Use youtube-transcript-api (for coders)

If you're comfortable with Python:

bash
pip install youtube-transcript-api

Then use it like this:

python
from youtube_transcript_api import YouTubeTranscriptApi

video_id = "VIDEO_ID_HERE"
transcript = YouTubeTranscriptApi.get_transcript(video_id)

for entry in transcript:
    print(entry['text'])

This pulls just the text. You can also convert it to .txt or .srt formats.
